Getting started

On first visit you see the registration bar. Enter a room name (shared with your team), your display name, and optionally tick "as scrum master". Then press Join or hit Enter.

♠ Scrum Poker
📷
Registration bar — enter room name and your display name. Pre-filled from your last session.
  • Room names are case-insensitive. Anyone using the same room name joins the same session.
  • Your room, name, and role are saved in localStorage and pre-filled on reload — rejoin with one click.
  • Two participants cannot share the same name in a room.
  • Only one Scrum Master is allowed per room at a time.
  • Optionally upload a profile photo (camera icon) — it appears on your chip and persists across sessions.

After the timer hits zero it shows blinking red overtime (+1s, +2s … +10s) for 10 seconds — so latecomers can see how far over time the team ran. It clears automatically and resets on New Round.

The timer runs server-side — all participants see the exact same countdown in real time.

Available durations: 30 s · 1 min · 1:30 · 2 min · 3 min · 5 min

Jira Integration

The Scrum Master can paste a Jira issue URL into the input bar below the team strip. It is broadcast to all participants as a plain link that opens in a new tab.

  • Paste the URL and click Load (or press Enter) to share it with the room.
  • Click ✕ next to the input to clear the link for everyone.
  • Participants without the SM role see the link immediately — no page reload needed.
ℹ️ Jira Cloud blocks embedding in iframes (X-Frame-Options: DENY), so the issue always opens in a new tab rather than inline.

Removing a participant

Hover over any team member's chip (other than your own) — it highlights and shows a remove cursor. Click to remove them after confirmation. The removed user is returned to the login screen and must rejoin manually.

Jira URL input

An input bar below the team strip lets the SM share a Jira ticket URL with the whole room. Participants see it as a clickable link.

Presence & Offline States

Connection dot

A small dot next to your name in the toolbar shows your WebSocket connection status:

Dot colourMeaning
GreyConnected — everything is live
RedReconnecting — trying to restore the connection

Participant offline state

When a teammate closes their tab or loses network, their chip is immediately dimmed and shows offline in red. They have a 30-second grace period to reconnect before being removed from the room.

Room offline indicator

When you lose connection, the room meta line in the toolbar shows · offline in red — so you know at a glance the session is disconnected without hunting for the status dot. The full "Offline — reconnecting…" overlay also appears.
